The new standard for government services

All new or redesigned transactional services going live after
April 2014 must meet the Digital by Default service standard.

A Digital by Default service is:

Agile

  • Owned by a single, suitably skilled service manager
  • Built and operated by a multidisciplinary team
  • Delivered using agile working practices
  • Continuously improved based on user feedback and robust research
  • Published following defined alpha and beta phases

Effective

  • Consistently reaching high levels of user satisfaction
  • Achieving a low cost per transaction
  • Consitently showing a high transaction success rate
  • Reaching, or following plans to reach, high levels of digital take-up

Open

  • Made up of code and content that is open and reusable
  • A user of open standards and APIs
  • Regularly written about by the team designing and building it throughout the life of the service

Usable

  • Able to meet the needs of its users
  • Usable by everyone able to access digital services
  • Accessible to those who can't use digital services
  • Device and platform independent
  • Seamlessly integrated with assisted digital channels
  • Part of GOV.UK in how it looks and feels
